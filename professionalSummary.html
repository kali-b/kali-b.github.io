Treyarch/Volt – Call of Duty: Black Ops 4

In June 2017, I began my journey at Treyarch with the amazing development team dedicated to making Call of Duty: Black Ops 4. I work closest with the Multiplayer Maps Development team, joining forces with an array of disciplines to bring the Black Ops multiplayer setting to life. Beginning with the earliest maps and continuing through our DLC season, I have dedicated my efforts to delivering cohesive, high-quality, and performant maps to players around the globe across major platforms (PS4/Xbox/PC).

As our development cycle continues, I further strive to generate a strong, streamlined, and agile team with my production partners – ready to deliver the best our studio has to offer in Black Ops 4 and beyond.

Outside of work, you can catch me on our PS4 and PC servers, or lurking on our community postings, taking our consumer feedback and working with the team to generate action items for MP maps present and future – targeting improved usability, content, and gameplay.

WG Cells (Dropforge Games) – Smash Squad

Starting in May 2016, I began work as a technical writer for WG Cells (formerly known as Dropforge games, prior to being acquired by Wargaming). This was my first industry experience!

While there, I re-established and managed the entirety of the company’s JIRA/Confluence documentation base. Through the creation of streamlined documentation protocols and templates the following became realities for the studio: document organization/conciseness, and cohesive presentation of information. Additionally, I generated new documentation for on-going and upcoming studio projects – including the studio’s final mobile releases Operation: New Earth and Smash Squad.

Unfortunately, WG Cells closed its doors in September 2016, thus marking the end of my employment with the fine people there. If you would like to read more about my first industry experience, you can check out my blog post Retrospective: My WG Cells/Dropforge Games Experience.
