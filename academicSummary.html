My Degree and Academic Experience

In May of 2017, I graduated with a BSCS in RTIS and Mathematics minor from DigiPen Institute of Technology. This degree, formally known as the Bachelor of Science in Computer Science: Real-Time Interactive Simulation, was a 154-credit program at the time of my enrollment. The program trains students in the art of creating, designing, and presenting a variety of computer applications at the technical level. Through a combination of low-level and high-level language learning, students evolve into proficient programmers and software engineers.

Projects are an integral portion of the program. Students complete formally graded student-game projects on a semesterly, or yearly, basis. These projects typically approach an Episode Prototype or Beta testing stage, depending on the time frame allotted.

I worked on ten projects in my time at DigiPen, each with their own flavor, flair, and challenges. You can find out more about a few these in my project listing.

My experience on these student projects taught me a great deal in:

  • Production and Project management (Non-Technical and Technical)
  • Inter-Discipline Communications
  • User Experience and Interface Design/Testing
  • Software/Game Engine Architecture
  • Project Promotion and Marketing
  • Project Source Control
  • Project Documentation
  • Project Planning
  • Risk/Mitigation Management
  • Usability Testing
  • Technical Writing
  • Task Automation
  • Network/Audio/Interface Programming

My University

DigiPen Institute of Technology is a smaller, private, university located in Redmond, Washington. Referred to as Nintendo “U”, DigiPen was founded by President Claude Comair (co-founder of Nintendo of America).

The school is renowned for student successes in the gaming and technology industries. Nestled in the Pacific Northwest hills, DigiPen sits amongst software development giants such as Amazon and Microsoft, and several game studios such as 343, Wargaming, Bungie, and many more – making it a place of ample opportunity for upcoming software developers and content creators.
