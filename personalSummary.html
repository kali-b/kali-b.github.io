My Life and My Journey

Enamored with the entertainment industries from a young age, I had always dreamt of turning my small-town life into a bustling-city existence on the West Coast. I began playing video games when I was about 4 years old, and just really never stopped. My PC was my after-school haven, and arcades are where I made my best memories.

Throughout my childhood, I sought music and writing as possible career paths until my Junior Year of high school. My gifted studies teacher was an advocate for women in STEM, and it was at this time that she gave me a nudge in the direction of the sciences, something I had shown aptitude for. Following in my father’s footsteps, I started learning basic computer science – starting out in Java and Oracle-designed IDEs with his help.

I had grown up with computers, but it was only here where I began to understand how they did the things they could do. My interest grew, and as I approached college, I decided to see what opportunities I could find in the gaming industry – to see if I could make a career of a hobby that had always been there for me.

My father mentioned DigiPen as a possibility, and after a bout of research, I applied fairly immediately. Once accepted, I decided to pack my bags and move halfway across the country from Kansas to the beautiful Pacific Northwest. With the support of my parents, brother, professors, friends, two cats (Nyxi and Tornado), and a beefy pupperino (Stella), I graduated from DigiPen in May 2017 as one of two women in the RTIS program. Shortly after, I moved to L.A.

In between shipping games, I return to several hobbies of mine such as music, photography, writing, cooking, and being an avid theme-park goer. Los Angeles has been a treat to live in, and I have met many wonderful people who are helping me find my best self in all of this madness. I currently live on the West Side with a great partner, and two new fluffy babies of the cat variety named Mason and Morri.
