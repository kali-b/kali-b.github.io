My Life and My Journey

Enamored with the entertainment industries from a young age, I had always dreamt of turning my small-town life into a bustling-city existence on the West Coast. I began playing video games when I was about 4 years old, and just really never stopped. My PC was my after-school haven, and arcades are where I made my best memories.

Throughout my childhood, I sought music and writing as possible career paths until my Junior Year of high school. My gifted studies teacher was an advocate for women in STEM, and it was at this time that she gave me a nudge in the direction of the sciences, something I had shown aptitude for. Following in my father’s footsteps, I started learning basic computer science – starting out in Java and Oracle-designed IDEs with his help.

I had grown up with computers, but it was only here where I began to understand how they did the things they could do. My interest grew, and as I approached college, I decided to see what opportunities I could find in the gaming industry – seen as a beautiful blend of the right-brain and left-brain, and a dive into a hobby that had always been there for me.

My father mentioned DigiPen as a possibility, and after a bout of research, I applied almost immediately. Once accepted, I decided to pack my bags and move halfway across the country from Kansas to the beautiful Pacific Northwest. With the support of my parents, brother, professors, friends, cats, and a beefy pupperino, I graduated from DigiPen in May 2017 as one of two women in the RTIS program.

Now that I have more time on my hands after college, I am slowly returning to several hobbies of mine such as music, photography, writing, cooking, and being an avid theme-park goer – ready to experience what life has to offer me.
